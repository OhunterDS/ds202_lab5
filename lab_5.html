According to the FARS Analytical User’s Manual,DAY_WEEK of 1 goes with Sunday, 2 goes with Monday, all the way to Saturday.

This shows Saturday with more traffic accidents than any other day. The entire weekend has more accidents than the week days, this makes sense as there are probably more drivers during the weekend.

First join the accident data accident with glc.

choro <- accident %>% 
   select(STATE, ST_CASE, COUNTY) %>% 
   group_by(STATE, COUNTY) %>% 
   summarize(accidents = n()) %>% 
   left_join(glc, by = c('STATE', 'COUNTY'))

Load the map data.

counties <- map_data('county')
counties <- counties %>% 
   mutate(`State Name` = region, `County Name` = subregion) %>% 
   select(`State Name`, `County Name`, long, lat, group, order)

Next join all three together.

choro <- choro %>%
   full_join(counties, by = c('State Name', 'County Name'))
choro <- choro[order(choro$order), ]

Finally plot the graph.

ggplot(choro, aes(long, lat)) + 
   geom_polygon(aes(group = group, fill = accidents)) +
   xlab('Longitude') +
   ylab('Latitude') +
   ggtitle('Number of Accidents by County')

Most counties have less than 200 accidents. Some of the larger metropolitan areas, such as parts of Texas, Southern California, and Florida, the amount of accidents is higher, this is expected with a larger number of people.
